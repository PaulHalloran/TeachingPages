But first, what is the Earth System?

When we talk about the 'Earth System' we are referring to the interacting physical, chemical and biological processes that constitute on our planet.

Clearly this is a big topic, so in this module we limit ourselves to the processes going on on our planet today (for a wider view check out Earth System Science: The History of our planet), and limit ourselves to the natural environment and human 'forcings' acting upon in, rather than straying into human behaviour.

The key thing is that we're not thinking about specific aspects of Physical Geography in isolation (even though that is how aspects of it have to be taught), but as a system. To get into this mindset, do a bit of reading about 'system thinking', maybe starting here.

To kick things off and get us thinking about the Earth System in a hollistic way, we're going to collectively come up with some ideas about how different aspects of the system interact.

Start thinking a bit further about the atmosphere and oceans and how they contrast with the other components of the Earth System.
